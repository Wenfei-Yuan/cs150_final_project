## **Key Questions and Goals for User Feedback**

By conducting user feedback sessions, we would like to learn/confirm:

* How likely Gen Z would be to use fact-checking features on a news aggregator  
* How much Gen Z values seeing the political bias of articles  
  * The Google News team was curious about this question after our presentation last Friday.   
* What Gen Z wants to see when they first open a news aggregator after exiting a different source where they had read about news   
* Which parts of the news consumption experience Gen Z prioritizes or most often uses  
* What makes Gen Z trust a news aggregator  
* How likely Gen Z would be to use this particular news aggregator, and what it would take to retain their interest

## **Hypotheses** 

We would like to validate that Gen Z:

* Trusts news aggregators  
* Wants to read articles from sources of differing opinions in order to fact-check a topic  
* Would use a news aggregator if it emphasized fact-checking rather than news discovery  
* Would leave a social media platform to fact-check news they saw on the platform  
* Would use a news aggregator to fact check if the information was instantly accessable

We would like to disprove that Gen Z:

* Would never use news aggregators if they don’t include short-form video content  
* Only says they would fact-check on a separate platform, but wouldn’t actually do so in a real situation  
* Distrusts news aggregators  
* Trusts social media content more than legacy media outlets

## **Assumptions**

We are assuming that Gen Z:

* Has different media consumption habits than they wish to, and are open to working on changing those habits   
* Values staying up to date with current events  
* Uses a mix of legacy media and social media news  
* Values fact-checking  
* Want to know the political leaning of an article before they read it   
* Will find the most value in the fact-checking feature of our app  
* Will respond somewhat more aspirationally to our app than reality 

## **Study Methodology**

For the following studies, we will recruit 4 participants through peer and campus networks. We will aim to diversify our participant recruiting to target varying racial and gender demographics. Participants will be ages 18-25. Both studies will be facilitated by one group member to ask questions and clarify task confusion. The facilitator will take notes.

### *Study 1: Card sort for concept testing*

* Modality: in person  
* Length: 20-30 minutes  
* Goals: assess how users would categorize features, assess how users prioritize features  
* Logistics: Will take place across 3 days (3/23-3/25)  
* Tools: paper notecards to create the cards, paper & pen for notetaking, Google Sheets for data input  
* Sample task: Please rank these cards in order of relevance to your news consumption process. Which activities do you perform most often?  
* Sample task: Please categorize these cards in any way that makes sense to you. What categories are you creating and why? Why are you placing these cards in these given categories?


### *Study 2: A/B Testing for user preferences*

* Modality: in person  
* Length: 30-40 minutes  
* Goals: assess user preferences for features, assess ease of navigation  
* Logistics: Will take place across 3 days (3/30-4/1)  
* Tools: Figma for prototype, Zoom to record session, Notion for digital notetaking, Google Sheets for data input.  
* Sample task: Use this interface to see if this Instagram post (standardized, provided by us) is accurate.  
* Sample task: Use this interface to assess what political bias this publisher has. How do you know?

## **UX Metrics**

We will measure:

* Overall usability of the app’s happy path: [System Usability Scale](https://stuart-cunningham.github.io/sus/)  
* Consolidation of app’s happy path: amount of time and amount of clicks taken to reach from point A to point B  
* User’s satisfaction with app: a satisfaction score on a scale from 1-5  
* User’s likelihood of using the app: a satisfaction score on a scale from 1-5, a likelihood of recommending the app to a friend from 1-5, as well as qualitative anecdotes on where this app might fit into their lives

## **Use of Results**

We will use the results of our user feedback testing to iterate upon our design and provide recommendations to the Google News team. Our studies are designed in a way that we should be able to discern the features that provide the most value to users in the news aggregator experience. Thus, we will be able to synthesize a prioritized list of features that Google News could consider when developing their information architecture. This list would also inform our own information architecture, where we will make the highest priority items more accessible on the news aggregator interface. We will also be able to identify if our happy path is discoverable and usable, which will help us decide if we need more scaffolding or fewer steps in the flow. The clarifying questions that users ask in our concept tests will also define information to include in a potential onboarding flow.   
We will also use these results to validate our design decisions, which will strengthen our final presentation and argument to the Google News team and ensure that our designs are directly backed by user behavior.
